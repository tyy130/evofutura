Google's Project Genie 3 generates playable game worlds, raising questions for future of game development

January 30, 2026 2 min read via Escapist Magazine
ai-research robotics

For the last few months, gamers have largely let big dev studios know that AI is non-negotiable in games. From the fears of Larian’s ongoing use, ARC Raiders’ voice-acting debacle, and even Gen AI art in both Black Ops 6 and Battlefield 6, it is a bit wild. But now, Google has unleashed Project Genie 3 for US AI Pro subscribers.

The tool is your standard prompt model, requiring players to enter a prompt. It will attempt to generate a 3D space to explore based on your queries. The demo on the site shows simple things, like a dog wandering around a front porch. But implementing it in a gaming setting seems to create some super interesting worlds. It’s creating video game-like levels with physics, models, graphics and other content.

Project Genie is making games by recognizing triple-A titles

An X account called fofrAI has the subscription, and highlights some prompt optimization for the Project Genie 3. It’s able to make mini games out of the prompts for exploring and platforming-style games.

It gets to the point where users can highlight video game assets, and it can learn and train in a contextualized way. One user even posted a video of Zelda’s incoming Breath of the Wild-style gameplay.

It’s uncanny.

The user did it by getting Grok to make a Zelda image, for some reason. Throw that image into Project Genie, and off it went, making a level based on that image. The level features Zelda-like gliding mechanics, and its art style too.

There’s also some other gaming mechanics involved with the likes of GTA-style worlds, where players travel around the map in helicopters. Other game examples were from Dark Souls, with a similar UI, respawn when you fall off mechanics, and more.

I reckon you could probably prompt Anthem-style gameplay if you wanted to, especially with Anthem being dead at the moment for that thrill visual flying style.
