Belton ISD Launches Unified Robotics Team, Advances to State Competition

January 31, 2026 2 min read via KWKT - FOX 44

Temple, TX (FOX 44) – Belton ISD just flipped the switch on something special. The district launched its first unified robotics team, the Circuit Breakers. Marking a major milestone in inclusive STEM education.

Representing Lake Belton High School, the team placed second at the Texas Special Olympics Unified V5RC event on January 21st in San Antonio, earning a spot in the state competition.

The Circuit Breakers bring together students with and without disabilities, supported by student mentors, all working together as one team.

“The academic learning, they get to learn new things within the engineering and STEM field that maybe they haven’t seen before,” Robotics and Engineering Teacher, Jessica Persilver said. “It brings a lot of the technical how to build things together, and soon we’re going to incorporate the coding part as well. And I think for the social and emotional day, they’re getting to work with people they haven’t before.”

Educators and administrators say the program builds teamwork, problem-solving, confidence, and inclusion.

“Just some learning opportunities to how it all works, and how it’s put together,” Circuit Breakers Teammate, Jacob Harris exclaimed.

The team says earning second place at regionals was a big deal, and even with a few nerves, they’re proud of how they performed.

“Kind of, uh, pretty cool to do something like that,” Circuit Breakers Teammate Reese Kirkland said. “It’s kind of like hard when you, when getting nervous, when competing with other students from other schools.”
