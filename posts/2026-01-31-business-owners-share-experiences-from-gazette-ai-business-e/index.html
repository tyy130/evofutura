Business owners share experiences from Gazette AI business event

January 31, 2026 2 min read via Brentwood Live

Business leaders gathered at Stratstone Ferrari in Colchester for an exclusive Gazette marketing event exploring the growing impact of artificial intelligence on business and marketing.

Hosted by LOCALiQ, Newsquest’s digital marketing agency, the evening brought together organisations from across Essex and Suffolk to discover how changes such as Google’s AI Overview, AI Mode and GA4 are reshaping how customers search for and engage with businesses online.

The Walsh family (Image: Newsquest Reporter)

Among those attending were representatives from Dunmow Crematorium, run by the Walsh family through Michael J Walsh Funeral Directors. David J Walsh and his wife Liz attended alongside two of their children and described the evening as both informative and enjoyable.

They said they had a lovely time at the venue and found the tips shared during the presentations helpful for a family-run business navigating an increasingly digital landscape.

Jordan Kluth (Image: Newsquest Reporter)

Jordan Kluth, a solicitor at John Fowlers Solicitors, also attended the event and said the seminar was useful, offering valuable insight into how AI is changing marketing and visibility online.

Seena Shah, director at OWR Strategy, who works with multiple clients across different sectors, highlighted the importance of businesses engaging with AI now.

Seena Shah (Image: Newsquest Reporter)

She said: “I think if you’re not concerned about AI as a business, then you are mistaken.

“I am a marketing strategist for our business and I think tonight was wonderful.

“It’s a beautiful venue and we had a great time tonight learning about Google AI overview and AI mode.”

Chris Pollard (Image: Newsquest Reporter)

Chris Pollard, managing director at Dupliq, said the event helped demystify some of the challenges businesses face when approaching AI.
