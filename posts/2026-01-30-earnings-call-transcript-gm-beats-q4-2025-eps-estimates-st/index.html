Earnings call transcript: GM beats Q4 2025 EPS estimates, stock fluctuates By Investing.com

January 30, 2026 1 min read via Investing.com India
ai-research robotics

General Motors (GM) reported its fourth-quarter 2025 earnings, revealing an adjusted EPS of $2.51, surpassing the forecast of $2.24. Despite a revenue shortfall, the company’s stock saw significant fluctuations, with a pre-market rise of 6.89% before settling with a slight pre-market dip of 0.54%.

Key Takeaways

  • GM’s Q4 EPS of $2.51 exceeded expectations by 12.05%.

  • Revenue fell short, coming in at $45.29 billion against a forecast of $45.88 billion.

  • Stock experienced a pre-market surge but showed volatility shortly after.

  • Continued innovation in electric and autonomous vehicles was highlighted.

  • U.S. market share reached a decade high.

Company Performance

General Motors showcased robust performance in Q4 2025, with strategic initiatives in electric and autonomous vehicle technology. The company maintained its competitive edge, achieving the highest U.S. market share in ten years and leading in full-size pickups and SUVs. Despite a 5% year-over-year revenue decline, GM’s commitment to innovation and market adaptation remains strong.

Financial Highlights

  • Revenue: $45 billion, down 5% year-over-year.

  • Earnings per share: $2.51, up from the forecast of $2.24.

  • EBIT adjusted for Q4: $2.8 billion.

  • Full-year 2025 EBIT adjusted: $12.7 billion.

  • Adjusted automotive free cash flow: $10.6 billion.

Earnings vs. Forecast

General Motors reported an EPS of $2.51, significantly beating the forecast of $2.24, marking a 12.05% surprise. However, revenue fell short of expectations, with actual figures at $45.29 billion compared to the anticipated $45.88 billion, a negative surprise of 1.29%.

Market Reaction

GM’s stock experienced a pre-market rise of 6.89%, moving from $79.43 to $84.9. However, following the earnings announcement, the stock showed volatility, with a slight pre-market decline of 0.54%, closing at $85.79. This movement reflects investor sentiment balancing the EPS beat against revenue shortfall and broader market conditions.
