ET GenAI Hackathon: Shaping Enterprise GenAI in India

January 31, 2026 1 min read via Economic Times

As generative AI moves firmly into real business use, The Economic Times’ ET AI Hackathon has become a space where industry and builders engage early on ideas meant for real-world application. With adoption accelerating across sectors, the platform offers organisations a timely opportunity to collaborate while the direction of enterprise GenAI is still being shaped.

Generative AI is now considered an ongoing reality rather than a future trend. Within Indian enterprises, it has already become a part of daily discussions concerning productivity, decision-making, and expansion. What has shifted most clearly is the tone. Curiosity has transformed into purpose. Leaders are now questioning not what GenAI is capable of, but what it ought to do, and how quickly it must begin providing value.

As this change takes effect, one thing is evident: significant advancements will not arise from separate initiatives. The most viable concepts are arising when those developing the technology engage closely with individuals who grasp business realities. Platforms facilitating this exchange early, before methods become established, are gaining significantly more importance than large, singular exhibitions.

This is where the ET AI Hackathon fits in.

The platform connects individuals developing with GenAI and organizations that are contemplating its integration into their operations. The focus is not on show or innovation, but on significance. Participants are urged to develop concepts that address actual limitations, established systems, data integrity, scalability, and the requirement for results that can be articulated and validated beyond technical groups.

For organizations interacting with the platform, the benefit frequently stems from closeness. Observing how teams tackle challenges today provides a glimpse into the future of GenAI capabilities.
