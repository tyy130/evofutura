Govt Launches New Curricula Focusing AI & Robotics, Art & Textile Design

January 31, 2026 1 min read via UrduPoint

ISLAMABAD, (UrduPoint / Pakistan Point News - 31st Jan, 2026) Minister of State for Federal Education and Professional Training (MoFEPT), Wajiha Qamar, launched new curricula focusing AI & Robotics, Culinary Arts & food Sciences, Aesthetic Health Sciences, Art & Textile Design, Fundamentals of Food & Nutrition, and Home Management & Family Studies, marking a major step toward diversified and future-ready learning pathways.

She launched the new curricula during a two-day Interprovincial Curriculum Workshop (IPCW) held on 28-29 January 2026, organized here by the National Curriculum Council (NCC) Wing, MoFEPT, which was successfully concluded.

The workshop brought together around 120 curriculum experts and education stakeholders from all provinces, AJK, and Gilgit-Baltistan, including over 36 female experts, reflecting strong interprovincial representation and inclusive participation, said a press release on Saturday.

The workshop was organized by the NCC Wing team in collaboration with key stakeholders, including IBCC, FBISE, NRKNA, Allama Iqbal Open University (AIOU), and PIE, whose technical support and institutional cooperation significantly enriched the consultative process.

The Minister appreciated the inclusive and consultative approach of the NCC Wing. She emphasized learner-centered reforms focused on personality development and skills for an evolving world, reaffirming that there should be no discrimination among education systems in Pakistan.

Stressing that curriculum reform must go beyond timelines, she called for thoughtful, consultative, and context-based development.

Sharing her journey from Shakargarh, District Narowal, she highlighted how investment in education transformed the region into one with high literacy and strong human resources, and expressed her vision that every child in Pakistan should have access to quality education to change their destiny.
