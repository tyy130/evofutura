Morgan Stanley Sees Economic Growth In 2026, But A More Vulnerable Market

January 30, 2026 Source: FA Magazine

The U.S. economy is set up for another year of solid growth in 2026, according to Morgan Stanley. But it’s another story for the markets and stock pickers.

Speaking at the firm’s 2026 U.S. Markets Outlook media roundtable discussion, Morgan Stanley’s top investment leaders said regulatory relief, rising productivity, and massive capital spending tied to artificial intelligence should support economic growth. But they warned that elevated equity valuations could make the reward for risk less attractive.

“We can’t help but feel constructive about the trajectory of economic growth ahead of us,” said Vishy Tirupattur, Morgan Stanley’s chief fixed-income strategist, pointing to what he described as a rare alignment of policy support, productivity gains, and capital investment.

Morgan Stanley now expects U.S. GDP to grow 2.6% in 2026.

A central pillar of that outlook is capital spending. Tirupattur said the economy is “on the verge of a massive capex boost,” driven largely by data centers and power infrastructure needed to support AI.

Between 2025 and 2028, Morgan Stanley estimates that there will be roughly $3 trillion in global capex spending tied to data centers, and less than 20% of that spending has been completed so far. Hyperscale technology companies alone are expected to invest more than $500 billion this year.

“Much of the productivity improvement we expect is actually ahead of us,” Tirupattur said, noting that recent gains have largely been driven by pre-AI investments such as cloud computing.

That spending has already contributed to GDP growth and is expected to play a larger role in 2026.

Still, Morgan Stanley’s wealth management leadership cautioned that markets are already reflecting much of the optimism around growth and AI.
