MARKETING NEWS The age of the dupe: why counterfeits are reshaping brand value

January 30, 2026 2 min read via Merca2.0 Magazine

Piracy, clone, dupe… Counterfeiting has evolved and is no longer a clandestine act or reserved for the black market

Piracy, clone, dupe… counterfeiting has evolved and is no longer a clandestine act reserved for the black market. In 2026, so-called “dupes” – products that imitate the aesthetics or functionality of premium brands – have become normalized as part of aspirational and cultural consumption, especially among Generation Z. Statista’s Consumer Trends 2026 report confirms that this trend is not only growing, but also redefining the relationship between consumers and brands, creating opportunities, risks, and uncomfortable questions about the true value of branding in an increasingly fragmented environment.

What is a dupe and why is it winning over consumers?

A “dupe” (short for duplicate) is an inspired or imitative version of a popular product, usually one with high value or prestige. Unlike direct counterfeits, dupes do not necessarily use registered logos or brand names, but they replicate visual, functional, or sensory characteristics of leading brands.

The phenomenon has evolved both culturally and economically:

  • ⇒ Social media fuels its virality (e.g., TikToks featuring “luxury dupes worth buying”)

  • ⇒ Generative AI makes it easier to find or create dupes with precision

  • ⇒ Young consumers adopt it as a form of smart or rebellious consumption in response to traditional pricing

What data does the report reveal about the rise of dupes and counterfeits?

The Statista report presents striking figures on purchasing behavior in the United States:

  • ⇒ 36% of Gen Z consumers have purchased counterfeit luxury products

  • ⇒ 28% say they were influenced by social media to buy a fake

  • ⇒ 25% have used artificial intelligence to find similar or copied products …
