Sayram Lake is renowned for its natural beauty and cultural legends.

It's the wild west out there on the internet, these days, with AI-generated videos leading to a lawlessness of suspended belief, misinformation, and skepticism.

Valid, too. Some of these AI videos are just too real.

One video making the rounds recently has sparked the spidey senses of online denizens, making them wonder: Is it real or AI? It shows a frozen lake in China, with waves laden with shards of ice breaking on the frozen shore. And by all accounts, it is in fact, real.

The waves, although miniature, mimic a righthand pointbreak as they crash along the sloped shoreline. Surfing them, however, would be impossible - the water is filled with chunks of ice the size of hubcaps. Nonetheless, an impressive sight.

Reposting the original video, StreetArtGlobe wrote:

"We're at Sayram Lake (赛里木湖), in Xinjiang, China. This stunning alpine lake is known for its dramatic natural beauty, high elevation, and cultural significance.

"In winter - especially from December to March - the lake's surface can freeze and produce remarkable natural, raised ice chunks and shards that catch the light like crystalline sculptures."

They added: "Is it AI? Here's a hint... we don't post AI."

The original videos were posted on TikTok by user mumiangu16, showing more scenes of the frozen lake, and racking up millions of views.

For more about the lake, which is the largest and highest in China's Xinjiang autonomous region, here's the lore behind its formation from a tourist operation:
