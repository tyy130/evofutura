Responsible governance and privacy-by-design are crucial for gaining public trust in smart city projects.

Cities around the world are quickly adopting smart technologies to handle growing populations, complex infrastructure, and environmental challenges. Cameras have long been a part of urban surveillance. However, traditional camera systems mainly serve as passive recording tools, depending on centralized data centers to analyze events after they happen.

Edge AI cameras mark a significant change in this approach. By integrating artificial intelligence into the camera hardware, cities are turning visual data into real-time insights that improve traffic management, public safety, and environmental monitoring, going far beyond simple surveillance.

Edge AI cameras are imaging devices with processing capabilities that allow them to analyze video data locally, right at the network's edge. Instead of sending raw footage to centralized servers or cloud platforms, these cameras use AI models to identify patterns, objects, and anomalies in real time. They only send relevant insights or alerts, reducing bandwidth use and delays.

This ability to process data locally leads to faster decision-making and greater resilience. In smart city environments, where delays can have serious repercussions, edge AI cameras provide a practical solution for urgent tasks like traffic control, emergency response, and hazard identification.

Traditional urban surveillance systems mainly focus on recording and reviewing footage for security or investigative reasons. While this approach is helpful, it is mainly reactive. Edge AI cameras shift this dynamic by offering predictive and responsive capabilities. Instead of just capturing events, they interpret scenes as they happen.
