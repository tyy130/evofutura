More game developers are using generative AI year on year.(Image: Getty Images)

A recent survey has discovered that 36% of game developers are using generative AI in some way, despite negative sentiment towards the technology increasing in 2025.

The data comes from the Game Developers Conference, an annual event attended by video game developers around the world. Over 3,000 individuals working at development studios filled out the survey to map out industry trends - and the findings around AI use is striking.

The biggest find is that 36% of developers say that they are personally using generative AI in their work. This is a five point increase over the same survey in 2024.

As for how that's split across different parts of game studios, 51% work in business and finance, 41% work in production and team leadership, while 39% work in community, marketing and PR.

Although usage appears to be going up, sentiment towards AI use is increasingly negative versus the year before. Only 13% of survey respondents claimed AI was having a positive effect, while 30% claim it's having a negative impact.

The number of developers who think AI is impacting game development negatively is a 12 point increase over 2024. Meanwhile, the number of respondents who consider AI use to be positive has decreased by eight points.

As for why developers are concerned about the technology, the most common reasons are cited as being, "intellectual property theft, energy consumption, the quality of AI-generated content, potential biases, and regulatory issues."

While AI use is on the rise, it's still not mandatory at the vast majority of development studios. When asked if AI was being forced upon employees, only 9% of the survey said it was mandated.
