* Three Volusia County Council candidates aligned with the slow-growth movement shared similar views at a recent forum.

* The candidates expressed opposition to large developments, a proposed motocross facility, and potential AI data centers due to water usage concerns.

* They criticized the current council's spending, particularly cutting arts funding while pursuing a multi-million-dollar motocross project.

NEW SMYRNA BEACH - Slow the Growth Volusia, a social-media group, hosted a Jan. 29 forum where the three County Council candidates who participated pretty much agreed on everything.

They want large-scale, sprawling developments reigned in, more protection for groundwater, and county tax dollars spent on priorities other than a proposed motocross facility.

Between 40 and 50 people attended the town hall.

The candidates, Bryon White, Wendy Anderson and Mike Poniatiowski, are all affiliated with the slow-growth movement, which has picked up steam in Volusia County in the midst of several floods and a few hurricanes, like Milton in 2024.

On protecting the Florida aquifer

Other issues have animated some residents. Perhaps the biggest applause line in the auditorium of the New Smyrna Beach Regional Library came from Anderson when she spoke out against the possibility of any large data center coming to Volusia County.

"I would be highly opposed ... I mean, laying-down-in-the-middle-of-the-street strongly opposed to having any big data, AI data centers coming into our county," Anderson said. "Those are huge energy and water users. I talked to the public utilities director for the county and he said, 'We don't have enough water in this county to support those.'"

White said water quantity is already a "huge problem and it's only going to continue to get worse," as Florida has tapped its aquifer for 100 years, and the growth in the state of 23 million has only intensified that reliance.
